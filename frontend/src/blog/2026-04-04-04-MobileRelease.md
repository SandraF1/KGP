#  The big news

The Koine Greek Project mobile app is in development!  Here I document my approach in the design stages of the app.

# Mobile app vs web app

I admit that the web app was very much based on standard grammars and textbooks.  The grammar translation method of learning New Testament Greek has not changed in years if not decades.  I did attempt to incentivise learning by making this more interactive via different types quizzes (multiple choice, drag and drop, ordering).  But the mobile app demanded a significantly different approach.

# The constraints and affordances of mobile technology

The biggest constraint of mobile technology for me is the size of the device, followed by the increasingly short attention span given to mobile apps.  However, it also presents opportunities to create bite-size and highly interactive lessons.  Inspired by modern language apps, I decided to explore a more linear, step-by-step approach to online language learning.

# The features of the KGP mobile app

The basic format of the app is:  

- Grammar/explanation
- Vocabulary
- Parsing
- Stories (translation)

Interaction is 'baked into' the navigation.
